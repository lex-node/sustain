Blockchain Governance
  • Szabo's Blockchain Governance Trilemma
  • Against On-Chain Governance
    • Against On-Chain Governance by Vlad Zamfir

      • tldr: On-chain governance means that blockchain nodes automatically upgrade when an on-chain governance process decides on an upgrade. Off-chain governance is better because node operators must consciously decide whether to install a hard fork and thus they are necessary participants in governance. On-chain governance moots node operator participation. Thus, with on-chain governance an important check on developer and tokenholder power is lost. Users !== token holders, their interests may diverge, and in general blockchains should benefit the public in general, not merely token holders. Thus plutocracy based on token ownership is undesirable.

    • Notes on Blockchain Governance Part 1 by Vitalik Buterin

      • tldr: "[A]rgue[s] hat "tightly coupled" on-chain voting is overrated, the status quo of "informal governance" as practiced by Bitcoin [and] Ethereum...is much less bad than commonly thought, that people who think that the purpose of blockchains is to completely expunge soft mushy human intuitions and feelings in favor of completely algorithmic governance (emphasis on "completely") are absolutely crazy, and loosely coupled voting as done by Carbonvotes and similar systems is underrated, as well as describe what framework should be used when thinking about blockchain governance in the first place."

    • Notes on Blockchain Governance Part 2: Plutocracy Is Still Bad by Vitalik Buterin
    • On Collusion by Vitalik Buterin

Technical Issues Affecting Blockchain Governance
  • The Verifier's Dilemma
    • Demystifying Incentives in the Consensus Computer

      • tldr: There is a dilemma for miners on blockchain networks with scripting capabilties â€” whether to verify the validity of scripted transactions or accept them without verification. Verifying scripts consumes computation resources and therefore delays honest miners in the race to mine the next block. This dilemma leaves open the possibility of attacks which result in unverified transactions on the blockchain. This means that some computation tasks outsourced to cryptocurrency-based consensus computers may not execute correctly

    • Vitalik's Reponse to Demystifying Incentives in the Consensus Computer

      • tldr: Ethereum miners have sufficient selfish incentive to refrain from setting a high gaslimit that blocks will not take very long to verify and the verifier's dilemma will not kick in.
