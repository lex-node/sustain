CryptoLaw Thought

For several years, I have worked to articulate a set of interlinked tools and concepts forming a kind of "cryptolaw philosophy".

My key ideas are:

  • native protocol tokens on such networks are shares of network equity representing an investment in a kind of un-ownable digital commons

  • smart contracts deployed on such networks should be combined with natural-language legal contracts under a synergistic "qualified code deference" methodology, which will enable parties to agree to abide by the results of smart contracts except in unusual circumstances

  • protocol tokens on such networks are appropriately regulated as securities, unless the network has become sufficiently decentralized; sufficient decentralization occurs when the tokens no longer represent investment contracts and no single party or group of affiliated parties objectively controls the network equity or means of block production on the network

The below articles and legal forms projects embody these ideas.

  •   Tokens as Securities Certificates - Tokens on public, permissionless blockchain networks function as transferable instruments ('certificates') representing traditional and decentralized securities.
